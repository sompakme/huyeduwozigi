Quality Sourcing and Processing

Hemp is a particularly greedy plant when it comes to growing. Its roots will happily suck up any and all things it finds in the soil, be it helpful nutrients or harmful chemicals.

That’s why finding farms pledged to organic growing practices is particularly important when investigating CBD or any hemp-based products. For our methods, quality sourced means hemp grown domestically, without pesticides, and preferably under organic farming practices.

Processing can be an equally tricky area for CBD products. Many extraction processes also involve harmful chemicals or otherwise less-than-appealing extraction agents. When You Should Use Dog Calming Treats

When considering how and when to use calming treats for dogs, cats, and other animals, there are generally two prevailing methods: The treats can be administered as part of a regular regimen or given to animals on an as-needed basis. Both approaches have their benefits.

Regular Regimen

CBD can take some time to build up in the body before reaching levels that offer the desired effects of the product. And once a certain baseline has been reached, it can become even more impactful, making the body more responsive to CBD and other cannabinoids.

Using a regular CBD regiment can help build—and maintain—the presence of CBD in an animal’s system. This can be a beneficial method for animals with chronic stress or other health issues, giving them the best chance of receiving all the best benefits of the best calming treats for dogs.

Again, you should always consult with your veterinarian about the specifics of your animal before starting them on a CBD regimen.

As-Needed Basis

Offering CBD treats as needed is also an excellent option for many pets and pet owners.

This type of treatment is beneficial if you know your dog has particular stress triggers, such as:

  • Having company over
  • Going for rides in the car
  • Hearing loud noises

If you know one of these situations is coming up, offering your up calming treats for dogs can be an effective and proactive way to help them get through the experience.

How quickly the calming treat takes to kick in depends on the strength of the CBD, the ingestion method, and the size of the dog, cat, or animal, among other factors. However, many companies claim their products start to work between 30-60 minutes.

What is known is that dogs have a faster metabolism than humans, making the CBD run through their system more quickly, with studies finding CBD staying in an animal’s system for anywhere from 4 to 9 hours.

Daily Use?

Many people wonder whether they can give their dogs calming treats every day.

While a regular CBD regimen can undoubtedly benefit pets, many experts are wary of recommending dosing as a daily routine.

CBD has proven itself safe in many studies on humans and lab animals, though studies focusing on dogs, in particular, are still very rare, making some vets hesitant to suggest it.

Still, while falling short of making a full endorsement for daily use, the American Kennel Club has noted that evidence for the benefits of CBD on dogs is “compelling.”

As always, the best recommendation for how and when to use calming treats for dogs will come from your veterinarian.

Benefits of Using Calming Treats for Pets

There are many potential benefits of using calming treats for dogs, cats, and other pets.

First and foremost is the stress relief such a routine can offer your animal. Stress is equally as uncomfortable for pets as it is for pet parents—and perhaps even more so, without the same ability to understand why certain situations are happening.

Also, like in humans, stress can go on to manifest physically in dogs, leading to:

  • Pacing and shaking
  • An increased heart rate
  • Compulsive licking or chewing
  • Diarrhea and other digestive issues
  • Shedding
  • Destructive behavior (e.g., tearing apart couch cushions, biting)

Keeping pets calm can help combat all those physical ailments that come with anxiety.

A calmer pet is also typically better at socializing, which can be hugely beneficial for both animals and humans.

Positive socialization is a crucial step for puppies, helping them build a happy and healthy foundation, and rescue animals who may be struggling with challenging issues like PTSD.

Other Ways to De-Stress Your Pets

Of course, natural supplements aren’t the only calming aids for dogs.

Exercise

Exercise has long been one of the best and most consistent channels for stress in both dogs and humans. If your animal is showing signs of anxiety, physical activities like walking, running, or even playing a game of fetch can help them utilize the excess energy that would otherwise be spent on stressing them out.

Training

While it may sound counterintuitive, having your dog sit and respond to routine commands can also help.

Just as with people, a routine can be hugely comforting for dogs, and having them run through their known set of tricks can both distract them from whatever is making them anxious and help provide a sense of normalcy.

Removing the Stressor

Understandably, if a particular situation seems to be stressing your dog out, removing them from that situation is the best and most immediate way to deal with their anxiety. Though, experts warn against the instinct to pamper them too much in these instances. If you want to give them a treat, it’s better to ask them to sit, stay, or shake first.

FAQs

The worlds of CBD and better pet health can both be confounding. Combine them, and things can get even more complicated.

Naturally, there are many questions people might have about using calming treats for dogs.

Is CBD safe for dogs?

As there are currently no formal studies examining the health effects of CBD on dogs, it can be difficult to understand whether or not CBD is in fact safe for dogs.

However, more than 23,000 tests on humans and lab animals have been performed, with most of the results yielding positive or neutral effects. In a 2017 World Health Organization report, it was noted that CBD in its pure state appeared “safe” and “well-tolerated” by animals.

Though, the compound is not without its potential side-effects, including:

  • Dry mouth
  • Lowered blood pressure
  • Drowsiness

The American Kennel Club warns that CBD could become toxic if an animal is given too high a dose.

Do CBD treats work for dogs?

Again, official test results are lacking, though a wide variety of anecdotal evidence points to CBD’s effectiveness in dogs, cats, and other animals, particularly for treating physical pain and helping curb anxiety (including separation anxiety in dogs).

The compound also shows particular promise for aiding or abetting:

  • Arthritis
  • Itchiness
  • Seizures

CBD is even believed to help prevent certain forms of cancer.

What other pets can take CBD calming treats?

Pet calming treats are most commonly associated with dogs and cats, but all types of animals can potentially benefit from CBD.

The compound works by interacting with a part of the body called the endocannabinoid system (EDC), a network of sensors in charge of keeping the body’s internal systems in balance, among other responsibilities. So long as an animal has an endocannabinoid system, it can potentially benefit from CBD.

And mammals aren’t the only types of animals with an EDC. A recent study found that fish, reptiles, and birds all have the same kind of system. However, finding treats more appropriate for these types of animals may be very difficult.

Are CBD treats safe for puppies and kittens?

Depending on the dosage, calming pet treats can be safe for puppies or kittens. It is vital to dose your animal correctly and always start on the lower end. You never know how any compound may affect any given animal.

THC may also prove to be more toxic at lower levels for puppies and kittens, making a broad spectrum or isolate an ideal choice for younger pets.

As always, it’s essential to consult with your veterinarian before giving your puppy, kitten, or any animal a pet calming treat.
