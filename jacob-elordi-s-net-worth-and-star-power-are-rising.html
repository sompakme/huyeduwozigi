In just a handful of years, Jacob Elordi transitioned from a wide-eyed newcomer to a sought-after leading man.

Elordi moved to Los Angeles in 2017 to pursue an acting career. Elordi first grabbed attention when he played Noah Flynn, the bad boy love interest in The Kissing Booth film trilogy.

In a bid to avoid being typecast in teen heartthrob territory, Elordi yearned for more complex roles. He actively sought projects that challenged him as an actor.

This led him to HBO's critically acclaimed series Euphoria, where he portrays the volatile and toxic Nate Jacobs. This layered performance, a stark contrast to his Kissing Booth persona, garnered critical acclaim.

He then played a troubled Elvis Presley in the Presley biopic Priscilla, which is based on Priscilla's memoir Elvis And Me. His performance in the film was met with positive reception.

In the last quarter of 2023, Elordi starred in Emerald Fennell's psychological drama Saltburn as Felix Catton, a wealthy and charismatic British Oxford University student.

For the role, he earned a nomination for a BAFTA Award in the category of "Best Actor In A Supporting Role."

Elordi will play the lead role as Frankenstein's monster in the upcoming Netflix horror film Dr. Frankenstein, directed by Guillermo del Toro.

According to several sources, Jacob Elordi's net worth is estimated to be $4 million. This figure considers his film and television salaries, brand endorsements, and other potential revenue streams.

As he continues taking on increasingly prominent roles, this figure will likely continue on a healthy upward trajectory.

Let's explore the facets that have contributed to Elordi's net worth, which includes his film career, television roles, potential movie and TV salaries, and lucrative brand deals.

Jacob Elordi's Film And TV Career

Jacob Elordi's career began with minor roles, including an uncredited extra appearance in Pirates Of The Caribbean: Dead Men Tell No Tales.

His first significant acting role came in the Australian film Swinging Safari in 2018, where he portrayed Rooster.

In 2019, he starred in the second part of the anthology horror film The Mortuary Collection, playing a 1960s frat boy who finds a creature growing inside his stomach after he has unprotected sex.

The turning point in his career arrived with the Netflix romantic comedy The Kissing Booth, where he portrayed the charming bad-boy jock, Noah Flynn.

The franchise, spanning three films, saw immense popularity and recorded high numbers on Netflix streams.

After the Kissing Booth trilogy, Elordi faced financial struggles and the looming expiration of his visa; he took a leap of faith and auditioned for Euphoria, viewing it as his final shot before returning home.

He was then selected to play Nate Jacobs in the show. The show's raw exploration of teen struggles received critical praise, and his nuanced performance continues to earn high praise.

In a bid to stretch his dramatic muscles, Elordi played a supporting role in 2 Hearts, a poignant romantic drama.

While the film's box office performance was modest ($1.4 million), it showcased Elordi's ability to handle emotionally complex characters.

He featured in the lead role as Elvis Presley in Sofia Coppola's Priscilla, which is based on Priscilla's memoir Elvis And Me.

Priscilla is Elordi's highest-grossing project, earning $29 million worldwide.

Elordi starred in Emerald Fennell's psychological drama Saltburn as Felix Catton, a wealthy and charismatic British Oxford University student. The role earned him critical praise and a BAFTA nomination.

Saltburn grossed a worldwide total of $21.1 million. Recent years have seen Elordi secure increasingly prominent film roles.

He appeared in the erotic thriller Deep Water, alongside Ben Affleck and Ana de Armas.

Elordi's Salaries From Movies And TV Shows

The specific salary figures for Elordi's roles are often not publicly disclosed, but industry standards and reported figures offer some insights.

His breakout role in The Kissing Booth trilogy likely placed him within the standard range for supporting casts in streaming productions.

This could be anywhere from $100,000 to $300,000 per film, depending on the project's budget and negotiation leverage.

In an interview with GQ, Jacob disclosed that he had a mere $400 to $800 remaining in his bank account before auditioning for Euphoria, which he considered his final chance before returning to Australia to replenish his funds.

He recounted sleeping in his car until an HBO producer arranged accommodation at The Standard in West Hollywood.

Elordi's critically acclaimed performance in Euphoria on HBO likely commanded a higher salary. Lead actors on HBO dramas can earn anywhere from $150,000 to $1 million per episode.

While Elordi's exact pay is not confirmed, reports suggest he was within the six-figure range by the show's second season. For his recent film roles, sources report Elordi earned $320,000 for his BAFTA-nominated performance in Saltburn.

Similarly, Elordi received a respectable sum of $150,000 for his supporting role in Deep Water.

Elordi's portrayal of Elvis Presley in the biopic Priscilla proved to be his biggest paycheck so far, earning him a sizable paycheck of $500,000.

These figures demonstrate a clear upward trajectory in Elordi's compensation as he takes on more prominent roles.
