Meet Chef Jos Andrs 3 Daughters: Explore Spain with the World-Renowned Culinary Family

 Posted on September 13, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  716 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

With José Andrés and Family in Spain set to premiere soon, fans of the world-renowned chef are eager to get to know him on a more personal level. 

In the show, José will be joined by his three daughters: Carlota (23), Inés (21), and Lucia (18). A resilient family, they’ve been living together as a unit in Bethesda, Maryland since 1997 with their parents Patricia Tichi Fernández and Jose Andres. 

The exciting new series makes it possible for fans across the globe to see Chef Jose explore his Spanish roots while taking them along for the ride.

His married life explored 

Patricia and José celebrated their 29th anniversary of marriage in September 2020. Patricia is originally from Spain and holds a degree from The University of Maryland. 

She was working in the commercial department of an Embassy when she met her husband at the Café Atlantico, Washington D.C., where she was enjoying time with her friends. Since then, they have been inseparable. 

In 2018, Andres lovingly praised his wife saying that Patricia is an organized and sensible person who has always given him great support and love over the years. 

Indeed, it can be said that they share an unbreakable bond and an admirable relationship that is built on true love and trust.

All about his daughters 

Carlota Andrés is the first daughter of Jose Andrés and his wife, Patricia Jaix. After graduating from the Stone Ridge School of the Sacred Heart in 2017, Carlota enrolled in the NYU Stern School of Business to pursue a degree in business, management, and food studies. 

During her collegiate career, she interned at multiple renowned businesses such as Beefsteak, Cava, Lasso Ventures, and NYC Startup. Since graduating in May 2021, Carlota currently works at a food technology start-up named Wonder as a senior associate. 

She will also be appearing on her father’s new culinary show José Andrés and Family in Spain to share her wealth of knowledge with viewers around the world!

Middle daughter-Inés Andrés

Exploring a life of service and dedication to helping others, 21-year-old Inés mirrors her elder sister in her countless volunteer hours with the World Central Kitchen and an excursion to Poland during the war. 

She has already accomplished much and is set to graduate in 2023 from Georgetown University Walsh School of Foreign Service, having studied International Politics with a double minor degree in French and Anthropology. 

Other than that, she has vast firsthand experience wherein she was able to witness the effects of violence on innocent lives. Her determination and commitment to creating a better world are inspiring.

Youngest daughter-Lucia Andrés

The dearly anticipated commencement of the Stone Ridge School of the Sacred Heart this June marked an incredible moment of success for its class of 2022 and a time of immense pride for jubilant parents — none more so than Chef José, whose beloved youngest daughter Lucia was graduating. 

Despite the tumultuous year that preceded the commencement, Chef José declared in his inspirational speech to the graduates that what they had accomplished was “a super big deal.” He urged them to embrace their newfound independence and shared his insights on life after graduation. 

It was certainly a special occasion that both Lucia and her father will cherish fondly in years to come!

Chef Andres is all set to take his viewers to explore his childhood memories 

Chef José Andrés will take viewers on a journey to explore his childhood homeland of Spain in the newest series premiering on Discovery+, “José Andrés and Family in Spain.” 

Throughout the show, fans will be transported to some of the best cities in the world for culinary craftsmanship such as Madrid, Barcelona, Lanzarote, and his birthplace of Asturias. 

Andrés will be joined by his three daughters – Carlota, Inés, and Lucia – who will team up with their father to cook regional dishes that have greatly inspired his work over the years.  

Along this culinary adventure, they will meet friends and experienced chefs who bring light to all that is great about Spanish cuisine. 

By delving into even the most intimate details of Spanish cooking culture presented in this docuseries, viewers are sure to be left with a newfound appreciation for traditional and modern fine dining from one of the world’s leaders in exquisite gastronomy.
