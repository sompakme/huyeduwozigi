Early Life

Rick Nielsen, the legendary guitarist of Cheap Trick, was born on December 22, 1948, in Elmhurst, Illinois. Growing up in a musical household, Nielsen’s parents were both opera singers, instilling a love for music in him from an early age.

Notably, Nielsen’s parents also owned a music store in Rockford, where he spent much of his childhood. It was in this music store that Nielsen discovered his passion for playing multiple instruments and honed his musical skills.

Initially, Nielsen’s journey in music began behind the drum kit, where he played for six years. However, his talent and versatility soon led him to explore other instruments. From keyboards to guitar, Nielsen embraced his musical curiosity and embarked on a path that would lead to his incredible career in rock music.

Career

Rick Nielsen’s career in music has been marked by his involvement in various bands and his extensive discography. He started his musical journey playing with bands like The Phaetons, The Grim Reapers, and Fuse. However, it was in 1973 that Nielsen found his breakthrough when he co-founded the legendary rock band Cheap Trick.

Cheap Trick, consisting of Rick Nielsen, Bun E. Carlos, Tom Petersson, and Robin Zander, quickly gained recognition in the music industry. With Nielsen’s songwriting skills and the band’s energetic performances, Cheap Trick became known for their catchy hooks and anthemic rock sound.

Throughout his career with Cheap Trick, Rick Nielsen has written many of the band’s hit songs, leaving an indelible mark on their discography. The band has released a staggering 20 studio albums, including classics like “Dream Police,” “Surrender,” and “I Want You to Want Me.” Their music has resonated with audiences worldwide and continues to be influential in the rock genre.

In addition to his work with Cheap Trick, Nielsen has also collaborated with various artists and lent his musical talents to over 20 other albums. His guitar skills and distinctive sound have made him a sought-after collaborator in the music industry. Whether it’s through his own bands or collaborations, Rick Nielsen’s contributions to music are diverse and impactful.
