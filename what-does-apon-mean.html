What’s the difference between upon and Apon?

Now in the modern spellings, the word or preposition “apon” is spelled as “upon.” They are actually the same word, only the use has been in different eras. … “Apon” and “upon” are the same word; both are prepositions which are used to show the relationship of the pronoun or noun in a sentence with other words.

When to Use enter or enters?

As for your original question, you would use the singular enters. It’s true that when you have multiple subjects, the verb would be plural, as in “A man and a woman enter the room.” But when your subject is preceded by every (or each, either, neither) you use the singular. Everybody enters the room.
