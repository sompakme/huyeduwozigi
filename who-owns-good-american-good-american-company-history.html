Who Owns Good American? Good American Company History

 Posted on September 17, 2024  |  14 minutes  |  2794 words  |  Larita Shotwell

In October 2016, Good American was co-founded by Emma Grede and Khloe Kardashian. Emma Grede, the CEO and Co-Founder, is a serial entrepreneur and strong advocate for diverse and inclusive representation in the fashion industry. Khloe Kardashian, a co-founder, primarily focuses on design and marketing for the brand.

Before Good American, Emma Grede founded ITB, a talent management and entertainment marketing agency. She has also co-founded other successful ventures like SKIMS with Kim Kardashian West and Safely, a plant-powered cleaning brand.

Good American’s vision is to empower all women to celebrate their bodies with confidence by offering high-quality designs in all sizes. The brand is renowned for its commitment to inclusivity, providing a wide range of sizes from 00 to 24 Plus.

The Journey of Emma Grede

Emma Grede, the CEO and Co-Founder of Good American, has had an impressive journey in the fashion industry. With a solid background in fashion show production and talent management, Emma’s entrepreneurial spirit and drive have propelled her to great success.

Emma’s path to success began when she attended the prestigious London School of Fashion. There, she honed her skills and laid the foundation for a career dedicated to making a mark in the fashion world.

After completing her education, Emma ventured into fashion show production, gaining valuable experience in the industry. This hands-on experience allowed her to develop a keen eye for trends, aesthetics, and what resonates with consumers.

Not content with just production work, Emma decided to take her career to the next level by founding ITB, a talent management and entertainment marketing agency. At ITB, she worked with high-profile clients and brands, building connections and establishing herself as a force to be reckoned with.

The success of ITB caught the attention of the industry, leading to its acquisition by Rogers & Cowan. Emma’s role in the company’s growth and ultimately her exit showcased her exceptional business acumen and ability to navigate the ever-changing fashion landscape.

In addition to her role at Good American, Emma has also made a name for herself as an investor in multiple start-ups. Her entrepreneurial spirit extends beyond her own brand, as she actively seeks opportunities to support and cultivate promising ventures.

Emma’s dedication and hard work have garnered recognition and accolades. She has been named one of WWD’s “Moguls in the Making,” highlighting her influence and impact on the fashion industry. Furthermore, she made history as the first Black female shark on the multi-Emmy Award winning series, “Shark Tank,” showcasing her expertise in business and entrepreneurship.

Beyond her professional accomplishments, Emma is also deeply committed to advocacy work. As the Chairwoman of Fifteen Percent Pledge, she actively supports women’s rights and female empowerment, ensuring equal opportunities for all.

Emma Grede’s journey is a testament to her passion, determination, and unwavering commitment to making a difference. Her influence as a CEO, entrepreneur, and advocate continues to shape the fashion industry and inspire others to follow in her footsteps.

Good American’s Inclusive Approach

When it comes to size-inclusive fashion, Good American is at the forefront of the industry. The brand is committed to inclusivity and offers a wide range of sizes, making fashion accessible to women of all body types. From size 00 to 24 Plus, Good American ensures that every woman can find stylish and well-fitting clothing that celebrates her curves.

One of the ways Good American embraces inclusivity is through their approach to photo shoots. Instead of using models of a single size, the brand features multiple sized e-commerce models, showcasing their clothing on a diverse range of bodies. This inclusive representation allows customers to see how the garments look on different sizes and encourages women to embrace their own unique beauty.

In addition to their range of sizes, Good American goes above and beyond by offering a unique size 15 option. This size was specifically created to accommodate women who fall in-between traditional size ranges, providing a perfect fit for those who often struggle to find jeans that flatter their body shape.

The inclusivity of Good American’s approach has resonated with women from all walks of life. Women appreciate the brand’s commitment to providing high-quality, trendy options for all sizes, allowing them to express their style and feel confident in their own skin.

Manufacturing Process and Global Sourcing

In order to ensure the highest quality for their customers, Good American jeans are manufactured in Los Angeles, USA. This commitment to local manufacturing allows for close supervision of the production process and adherence to strict quality control standards. However, Good American also sources some of its materials from international suppliers to create their exceptional line of denim.

Good American believes in transparency and communicates openly about their global sourcing practices. While the jeans themselves are produced in the United States, the brand sources certain materials from countries like Turkey. This global approach to sourcing allows Good American to access specialized materials and utilize the expertise of international suppliers, ensuring the best possible outcome for their products.

Emma Grede’s Philanthropic Work

Emma Grede, in addition to her success as a fashion entrepreneur, is actively involved in philanthropic endeavors. She is committed to creating positive change and empowering marginalized communities through her involvement in various organizations.

Women for Women International

One of the organizations that Emma Grede is deeply passionate about is Women for Women International. As an active trustee and board member, she supports the organization’s mission to empower women in countries affected by conflict. Women for Women International provides women with the necessary resources, education, and skills training to help them rebuild their lives and create sustainable change within their communities.

Baby2Baby

Emma is also involved with Baby2Baby, a nonprofit that focuses on providing essential items to children in need. As a board member, she plays a crucial role in ensuring that children have access to basic necessities and a safe environment to thrive.

Fifteen Percent Pledge

In her dedication to fostering diversity and representation, Emma serves as the chairwoman of the Fifteen Percent Pledge. This organization advocates for increased representation of Black-owned brands in businesses, helping to create a more inclusive and equitable retail landscape.

Through her active involvement in these organizations, Emma Grede continues to use her platform to make a positive impact on the lives of others and drive meaningful change in society.

Emma Grede’s Personal Life

Emma Grede, the CEO and Co-Founder of Good American, has a fulfilling personal life that complements her dynamic career. Born and raised in London, Emma now resides in Los Angeles, where she has built her empire and made a significant impact in the fashion industry. She is married to Swedish entrepreneur Jens Grede, and together they have four beautiful children.

Emma’s personal life serves as a constant source of inspiration and motivation. Emma’s dedication to creating positive change in the fashion industry is exemplified not only through her professional accomplishments but also through her role as a loving wife and mother.
